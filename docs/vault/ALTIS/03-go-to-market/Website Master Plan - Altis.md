# Altis Website Master Plan

## Product / Website Situation Summary

Altis is not just a boxing gym website.

It should present Altis as a **premium modern training brand** built around:

- **Discipline**
- **Transformation**
- **Elevated performance**
- **Community**
- **Structured coaching**
- **A future digital layer**

The website must do 3 jobs well:

- **Acquire**
  - Turn visitors into trial bookings or qualified leads

- **Convert**
  - Help people understand programs, trust the brand, and take the next step

- **Bridge to retention**
  - Prepare the brand for later integration with booking, member portal, and digital coaching

So the right approach is **not** “a beautiful gym website.”
It is:

- **A premium conversion-first brand site**
- **With a clean path to trial booking**
- **And architecture ready for future web app expansion**

# UX Diagnosis

## What Altis needs the website to communicate

- **[premium but real]**
  - High-value, disciplined, modern, not flashy

- **[boxing plus fitness]**
  - Not limited to combat identity
  - Broader performance and transformation positioning

- **[results plus structure]**
  - Clear programs, clear outcomes, clear journey

- **[human trust]**
  - Real coaches, real people, real place, real stories

- **[easy action]**
  - The visitor should know exactly what to do next

## Main risks to avoid

- **[too aggressive visually]**
  - Avoid cliché boxing-gym aesthetics, neon, chaos, macho intensity

- **[too generic]**
  - Avoid looking like any local gym with stock photos and vague promises

- **[too many choices]**
  - Homepage must not overwhelm with equal-weight options

- **[beauty without conversion]**
  - Visual quality must support trust and sign-up, not distract from it

- **[mixing marketing site and app too early]**
  - The public website and the future digital coaching product should be connected, but not merged prematurely

# Target Audience Prioritization

## Primary audience

- **[18–29]**
  - Wants transformation, energy, confidence, physique, structure, belonging

- **[30+]**
  - Wants health, sustainable training, better energy, discipline, stress relief

## Secondary audience

- **[parents for 7–14]**
  - Need safety, discipline, confidence-building, professionalism

- **[14–18 teens]**
  - Need challenge, identity, belonging, motivation

## Implication for website strategy

Do **not** design the website equally for all segments on the homepage.

Use this order:

- **[homepage lead narrative]**
  - Adults first: 18–29 and 30+

- **[secondary routes]**
  - Teens programs
  - Kids / parent-focused offer

This keeps the core experience premium and commercially strong.

# Homepage Master Blueprint

The homepage is the most important page.
Its job is to answer:

- Why Altis?
- Is this for me?
- Can I trust it?
- What should I do next?

- **Content**
  - Strong headline
  - One-line value proposition
